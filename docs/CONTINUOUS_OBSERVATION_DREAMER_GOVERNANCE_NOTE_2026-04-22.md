## Operational Doctrine

### 1. Continuous Observation

The system should prefer continuous observation over episodic guesswork.

That means:

- keep receipts
- keep snapshots
- keep visible read lanes
- keep before/after comparison surfaces

### 2. Bounded Dreamer

Dreamer is the anomaly and proposal lane.

Dreamer may:

- compare intended vs actual posture
- detect anomalies
- surface pressure on the watch board
- rank next inspections
- suggest bounded corrections

Dreamer may not:

- silently promote a behavioral rewrite
- outrank live theater truth
- bypass blackboard/query ordering
- act as a hidden controller

### 3. HOLD As Brake, Not Throne

HOLD exists to freeze a seam long enough for review.

Use HOLD when:

- anomaly pressure rises
- confidence drops
- policy or safety implications appear
- child-facing behavior changes are on deck

HOLD must stay:

- visible
- reviewable
- attributable
- bounded in scope

### 4. Continuity As Recovery Lane

Continuity preserves posture and return path.

It does not replace live truth.

The correct recovery order remains:

1. `continuity_restore`
2. `text_theater_embodiment`
3. `capture_supercam`
4. consult blackboard
5. `text_theater_snapshot`
6. scoped help/report/doc packets

### 5. Human Promotion Gate

If the system is being built as a stable substrate people can learn from, then promotion discipline matters more than spectacle.

Require explicit human review before:

- policy changes
- behavior changes with social or educational impact
- child-facing surface changes
- automation that changes operator authority boundaries

## Child-Facing Stability Rule

If the intended long arc is "a tectonic plate children can learn from," then the substrate must be:

- legible
- accessible
- reversible
- attributable
- non-manipulative
- stable under resets

That means:

- no revelation theater as a hidden controller
- no silent self-rewrite
- no opaque anomaly correction lane
- no dependence on transcript mystique
