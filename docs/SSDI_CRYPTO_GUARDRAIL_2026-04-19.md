# SSDI Crypto Guardrail 2026-04-19

## Purpose

Create a short-term protection plan for running the Technolit / creator-fee / treasury lane without accidentally wrecking SSDI or related benefits.

This is not legal advice.

It is an operating guardrail and preparation checklist.

## First Principles

### 1. SSDI is not the same as SSI

Do not mix them up.

If you are on SSDI only:

- the main federal risk is usually work activity / earned income / self-employment
- not every dollar of passive or unearned income automatically cuts SSDI

If you are on SSI, Medicaid, SNAP, housing support, or other means-tested programs too:

- different income/resource rules may apply
- this guardrail is not enough by itself

## What The Official Sources Say

### SSDI work rules

SSA says:

- you must report work activity if you get Disability
- in 2026, a Trial Work Period month counts when earnings exceed `$1,210`
- SSA also counts a Trial Work Period service month if you work more than `80 hours` in self-employment in a month
- after the Trial Work Period, the 2026 Extended Period of Eligibility earnings limit is `$1,690/month` for non-blind SSDI

### Benefits counseling

SSA funds WIPA counselors for exactly this situation:

- they give individualized advice about work and benefits
- they verify the actual benefits you receive
- they help with SSDI/SSI/health insurance interactions

### Tax

IRS says:

- most income is taxable unless exempted
- taxable income includes self-employment income and digital assets/cryptocurrency
- self-employment tax can apply to self-employment income
- a hobby is not the same thing as a business, but hobby income is still generally reportable
- whether something is a hobby or business depends on facts like profit intent, time/effort, and how businesslike the activity is

### Hobby versus business is not just what you call it

If this is truly a learning hobby:

- that can be a better fact pattern than an intentional profit-seeking business
- but the label alone does not control

IRS looks at factors like:

- whether you carry it on in a businesslike manner
- whether the time and effort show intent to make it profitable
- whether you depend on the income
- whether you expect future profit

SSA is also not limited by your label.

SSA's trial-work rule for SSDI uses the concept of "services," which includes activity done in employment or self-employment for pay or profit, or activity of the kind normally done for pay or profit.

### ABLE

If your disability began before age `46`, SSA says ABLE eligibility expanded effective `January 1, 2026`.

ABLE is most directly useful for SSI/resource management, but may still help as part of a broader disability-finance plan.

## Short-Term Safe Posture

### Do now

1. Keep project money separate from personal money.
2. Use separate wallets/accounts for:
   - personal living money
   - project treasury
   - creator-fee intake
3. Track your hours every week.
4. Track what money came from:
   - your labor / active operation
   - passive holding / investment
   - treasury funds not yet distributed to you personally
5. Save documents showing when money is treasury-controlled versus personally received.
6. Do not casually move treasury money into your personal account without a benefits/tax plan.

### Do not do yet

- do not assume an LLC magically protects SSDI if you are still actively working in the project
- do not assume crypto is invisible
- do not assume “utility” wording protects you
- do not treat SSDI like a debt you can simply repay later to erase work-activity issues
- do not assume a later donation to the government fixes reporting or overpayment problems
- do not wait until tax season or a benefits review to reconstruct the facts

## Operating Rule For This Repo

Until you have individualized advice:

- keep release manual
- keep your own distributions conservative
- prefer treasury retention over personal extraction
- treat active operation time as potentially countable work

That means:

- the system can pay others later through frozen epochs and reports
- but your own personal draw should be slow and deliberate until the benefits/tax posture is clear

## Immediate External Actions

### 1. Talk to a WIPA counselor

Ask for:

- SSDI impact of self-employment / creator-fee work
- whether hours, not just cash, could count against you
- whether any state/local benefits change the answer
- whether ABLE is useful in your exact case

### 2. Talk to a CPA or tax professional who understands crypto + self-employment

Ask for:

- treatment of creator fees
- treatment of treasury-controlled funds versus personal draws
- estimated tax planning
- recordkeeping requirements

### 3. If money gets real, talk to a benefits attorney

Especially if:

- distributions get large
- you are on concurrent programs
- you need formal structuring

## If You Want To "Pay It Back"

Keep these distinctions clean:

- if SSA later says you were overpaid, SSA has an official overpayment repayment path
- if you were properly entitled to SSDI at the time, there is not usually a normal “repay all my disability benefits” concept that fixes later work analysis
- if you want to make a patriotic or policy-motivated gift to the federal government, that is a separate Treasury donation path, not an SSA compliance path

So:

- repay an SSA overpayment if SSA determines one exists
- report work and income changes promptly so you do not create a bigger overpayment
- do not rely on a later gift/donation as your benefits strategy

## Questions To Ask WIPA

- I receive SSDI. If I actively run a meme-coin/creator-fee project, what exactly counts as work?
- If project funds stay in a treasury wallet and are not distributed to me personally, how is that treated?
- If I spend more than 80 hours in a month on the project, how does that affect SSDI even if I do not personally draw much money?
- Do I receive any benefits besides SSDI that have stricter income or resource rules?
- Should I be using an ABLE account for any portion of this money?
- What reporting should I do now so I do not create a later overpayment problem?

## Bottom Line

The biggest short-term danger is not “the coin got valuable.”

It is:

- you actively working a lot
- money and treasury facts getting mixed together
- no clean records
- no early benefits counseling
- assuming "it's a hobby" automatically solves SSDI or tax treatment

The safest next move is:

- separate accounts
- track hours
- keep payouts manual
- talk to WIPA before you personally take meaningful money out
