# Brotology Idea Capture Protocol

Date: 2026-04-24
Status: active
Scope: turning strong review/chat ideas into durable paper surfaces

## Purpose

Good ideas keep appearing in three places:

- operator riff
- reviewer prose
- cultural continuity residue

They do not become part of the project just because they sounded right in the moment.
They become part of the project when they are translated into a durable surface with a clear destination.

This note is the conversion rule.

## The Rule

An idea is not "on paper" until it has all five:

1. a named claim
2. a source class
3. a `verified / inferred / unknown` status
4. a destination surface
5. a next action

If one of those is missing, the idea is still theater residue.

## Source Classes

- `operator observation`
  A direct project observation or instruction from the operator.
- `review finding`
  A useful outside-model assessment, criticism, or recommendation.
- `cultural continuity artifact`
  A naming layer, ritual, mythic template, or Brotology branch.
- `mechanism determination`
  A bounded statement about how the system should work.
- `policy / exposure determination`
  A statement about what must or must not be exposed publicly.

## Destination Map

| Idea kind | Destination surface |
|---|---|
| mechanism determination | spec, dossier, primitive registry, motion-cycle note |
| risk or contradiction | spec risk section, review extract, or planning dossier |
| demo ladder step | operational surface, dossier, demonstrator brief |
| policy / exposure rule | layering policy, public overview, server/runtime gate |
| cultural naming / mythic template | `BROTOLOGISTS_LOG`, field manual, cultural note |
| reviewer acclimation guidance | `OPUS_ACCLIMATION_AND_RELAY_BRIEF` |

## Conversion Formats

Use the smallest honest format:

- `determination`
  When the claim is stable enough to guide later work.
- `brief`
  When the idea defines a bounded object, scene, or program lane.
- `note`
  When the idea is sharp but still intermediate.
- `log entry`
  When the idea belongs to cultural continuity and should not impersonate canon.

## What To Do When Opus Finds Something Good

When a reviewer returns a strong paragraph:

1. extract the smallest actual claim
2. classify it by source class
3. mark `verified / inferred / unknown`
4. choose the real destination surface
5. either:
   - promote it into the destination doc
   - or park it here until the destination doc exists

Do not leave the idea living only in praise, prose, or chat momentum.
