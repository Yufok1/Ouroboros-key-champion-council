## Purpose

State the economic law for the Atrai / brohando / civic-protection lane plainly enough that the rest of the docs do not drift into extraction cosplay.

## Bottom Line

The intended substrate is:

- value-broadcasting
- utility-producing
- reserve-bearing
- non-extractive by design

That does not mean "no money may ever exist."

It means the core loop cannot depend on trapping people into loss, rent, or hostage dependence.

## Three Registers

| Register | Short read |
|---|---|
| `caveman` | Do not build the big machine so the tribe must feed it forever while getting nothing back. |
| `operator` | Stored work must leave the system as real value to others, not just inward prestige or toll-taking. |
| `engineer` | The facility must show explicit receivers for power, water, communications, shelter, logistics, or cultural service before it claims economic legitimacy. |

## Core Law

```text
inputs -> transform -> public outputs
              |
              +--> maintenance reserve
              +--> safety reserve
```

Not:

```text
public inputs -> enclosed machine -> captive dependence -> extraction loop
```

## Allowed Economic Postures

Compatible funding or support modes may include:

- grants
- public utility partnerships
- research contracts
- donations
- bounded service revenue
- resilience and disaster-preparedness funding

The test is not whether money touches the system.
The test is whether the public is being turned into captive fuel.

## Non-Extraction Audit

Ask these five questions:

1. who pays in
2. who gets value out
3. who carries the downside risk
4. what remains public if the operator disappears
5. what part of the system becomes predatory under stress

If the answers all point upward into the machine and not outward into the community, the design has drifted.

## Preferred Outputs

The most legitimate outputs in this planning lane are:

- protection
- communications
- water or pumping support
- mobility / evacuation support
- educational and cultural broadcast
- resilience infrastructure

## Guardrails

1. No economic claim outranks mechanical honesty.
2. No giving claim counts unless a receiver is named.
3. No public-benefit claim counts unless maintenance and safety reserves are budgeted.
4. "Sky-giving" must not become branding for simple upward extraction.
