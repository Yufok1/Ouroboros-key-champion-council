# Source HOLD Open Surgery Doctrine - 2026-04-29

## Thesis

Source HOLD is the human authority boundary.

Champion Council, quines, cascade-lattice, Dreamer, Rerun, Arcade auth surfaces, and Atrai planning tools are advisory or infrastructural. They can prepare, model, expose, record, replay, and propose. They do not authorize themselves.

Humans are the living source of authority because humans live with the consequences.

## Open Surgery

Open surgery means system intervention must stay visible at the source level.

Required properties:

- visible source of the change
- explicit human/operator intent
- inspectable diff or artifact
- provenance receipt
- reversible backup or rollback path when practical
- no hidden second authority plane
- no autonomous escalation from advisory output into execution authority

The agent prepares; the operator runs. The agent may propose the cut, stage the patch, explain risk, and write receipts. The operator owns authorization.

## AI-Visible Surfaces

Anything an AI can look at is part of the open-surgery field.

This includes source files, logs, prompts, tool schemas, environment snapshots, screenshots, browser state, clipboard relays, blackboards, package metadata, provenance ledgers, Rerun streams, generated capsules, auth challenge states, and deployment configs.

For AI-visible surfaces:

- visibility is not authority
- observation is not consent
- read access is not write access
- generated interpretation is not proof
- cached context is not live state
- summaries are orientation, not source
- runtime behavior beats remembered claims
- secrets and credentials stay redacted or operator-held

If a surface can influence an AI decision, it needs source status:

- where it came from
- when it was observed
- whether it is live, cached, generated, or operator-provided
- what the AI is allowed to do with it
- what human authorization is still required

The clean rule:

No AI-observable surface becomes an authority plane just because the AI can see it.

## HOLD Boundary

HOLD is not a decorative safety label. It is the intervention latch where authority crosses from machine proposal to human decision.

HOLD must preserve these roles:

- AI proposes, diagnoses, simulates, and records.
- Human accepts, rejects, overrides, delays, or stops.
- Cascade records what happened but does not make it legitimate by itself.
- Quines preserve continuity but do not self-authorize continuation.
- Rerun shows the body of the event but does not become the event authority.
- Arcade/auth systems may broker credentials and permission challenges, but they must not bypass human consent.

## Atrai Mapping

Atrai is the ark faculty: a human-governed, community-bound mobile fortress for civic continuity.

It is sustained by mobile forces only:

- mobile shelter
- mobile energy
- mobile water
- mobile food and medical support
- mobile communications
- mobile compute and archives
- mobile repair and fabrication
- mobile training and governance surfaces

The mobile fortress is civic, not imperial. It carries community continuity without turning the stack into command sovereignty.

## Non-Goals

This doctrine rejects:

- autonomous machine rule
- hidden execution channels
- second authority planes
- unverifiable infrastructure changes
- provenance theater without human authorization
- funding rails that override community consent
- static empire logic disguised as resilience

## Operating Rule

When the system is uncertain, dangerous, expensive, civic, credentialed, or persistent, it must stop at Source HOLD.

The safe sentence is:

The system can prepare and show the cut; humans decide whether the cut is made.
